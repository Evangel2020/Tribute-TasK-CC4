General Chukwuemeka Odumegwu Ojukwu.

(November 4, 1933 - November 26, 2011)

A LEGEND

General Chukwuemeka Odumegwu Ojukwu

Time line of General Chukwuemeka Odumegwu Ojukwu.

  • 1933 - He was born in Zungeru, a community in the Northern part of Nigeria.

  • 1952 - He was admitted to Oxford University. Where he studied history, he graduated with degree of honors.

  • 1957 - He completed his officer training in England at the Officer Cadet School at Eaton Hall and was commissioned a second lieutenant. After attending the Infantry School in Warminister, England, the Small Arms School in Hythe, England, and the Royal West African Frontier Force Training School in Teshie, Ghana.

  • 1958 - He returned to Nigeria and was assigned to the Fifth Battalion in Kaduna.

  • 1960 - He was quickly promoted Once Nigeria had gained independence from Britain.

  • 1961 - He held the rank of major.

  • 1963- Odumegwu Ojukwu, as a lieutenant colonel, became the first Nigerian quartermaster-general in the Nigerian Army.

  • 1965 -He got his first independent command,he was assigned as commanding officer to the Fifth Battalion of the Nigerian Army in Kano.

  • 1968 - His article in Time magazine stated, "[Odumegwu Ojukwu] was a calm and reasoned voice pleading for a united Nigeria long after other powerful Ibos had angrily given up hope of preserving the union." Critics felt that because most of Odumegwu Ojukwu's inheritance from his father was in Lagos, he had a personal stake in keeping Nigeria together.

  • 1967 - He formally proclaimed the independent Republic of Biafra, during a reception in the regional capital of Enugu.

  • 1967- He received little sympathy or support from the international community,which made Nigerian forces to regained control of the midwest and had cut off Biafran access to the sea.

  • 1969 - Realizing that he would receive little foreign support, he appealed for United Nations mediation to obtain terms for a cease fire and to begin peace negotiations.

  • 1970 - The rebel nation collapsed.

  • 1982 - He justified his actions at the time by declaring, as quoted in Newsweek, "[W]hilst I live, Biafra lives." Odumegwu Ojukwu was invited back to Nigeria by Shehu Shagari of the Nigerian government.

  • 1993 - He publicly supported Nigeria's Republican Party because he thought it would be the best guarantor of eastern interests in national politics.

  • 2011 - On 26 November, Ikemba Odumegwu Ojukwu died in the United Kingdom after a brief illness, aged 78.
